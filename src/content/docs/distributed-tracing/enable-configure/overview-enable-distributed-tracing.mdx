To get started with the distributed tracing setup, take a look at the overview of the process, and then find the links below for specific steps with the telemetry tool you want to use.

## Overview

Setting up distributed tracing involves these three general steps: 

  ![The three steps to set up distributed tracing](./images/three-steps.png "three-steps.png") 

1. **Identify services:** As you get started, decide which services you want to keep track of in your traces: the more services you include, the better picture you'll get of your requests. 
    * Identify the endpoints, services, and systems that are included to complete this request.
    * Identify the language for each service you want to include in traces.  
2. **Instrument services:** Instrumentation is the process that allows services to participate in traces. The way instrumentation is handled varies between telemetry tools. For New Relic agents where automatic instrumentation is available, it's just a matter of installing the agent and setting some configurations. For other tools, such as OpenTelemetry, instrumentation may require some code in your service.
